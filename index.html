computing… folding histogram into accumulator
How this works: an accumulator is built from the file's modified time (with a confirmed 2-second correction applied, since Exl_win.exe's internal clock consistently runs 2 seconds behind whatever the OS reports), attribute flags, size, and the code-page-1251 byte-sum of its filename, then folded through a 360-bucket, golden-ratio-weighted histogram of the file's own bytes. The result is formatted the way Borland's Str(Extended) would, 10 of its digits are packed into 8 base-32 symbols, and a dash lands in the middle. Full derivation notes live in the original exlibrys.py reverse-engineering script this page is ported from. Matches depend on the browser reporting the file's true original Windows timestamp — copies, re-saves, or re-uploads may reset it, in which case use the override field above.
